How many times making the same mistake till I learn?

E018 | 28.04.25

Most probably you've experienced this before. You are trying to learn something new, and you make a mistake once, so you think that now that you have made the mistake, you've learnt. I mean, that's how we learn right? By making mistakes***.  Yes, and it’s true. But more on that later. So, you are surprised that when you are in a similar situation, as one that has learnt, you make the same mistake again.

Oh, maybe two times ain't too much for you. You make it the third time. So, you are frustrated with yourself, and you now believe there must be something wrong with you. You've been fooled more than twice. Double shame on you. You can’t learn dumb kid.

But is that true? Is it true that when you remake the same mistake it implies, even while you are trying to not do it again, that you are not learning. That you can’t learn. That when you learn about something you have been habitually doing is wrong for the first time, you should, if you can at all learn, immediately stop doing it? Nope. Making mistakes is how we learn, I said that again yes. For a reason.

Mistakes do not imply it has to be different mistakes. It could be the same mistake recurring, but all the while having a positive effect on you. You are becoming more and more conscious of the mistake, which consciousness will eventually reflect in action. How often you should make the same mistake before you don’t make it any longer does not define if you are learning or not.   

That you become more and more conscious and do it less and less frequently, which downward slope does not happen immediately also, does determine that.

Learning some new skill or unlearning some bad habit. Give ourselves more time than is needed (See Hofstadter's law) so you won't have to kick ourselves in the stomach too early for being too dumb to learn.

We learn by making mistakes — every mistake is a class.
 Some classes we have to attend more times than others before the lesson sinks in.
