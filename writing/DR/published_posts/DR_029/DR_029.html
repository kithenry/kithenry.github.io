On having a written why

E029|09.05.25

So yesterday I shared a post with a friend of mine. Today's morning after I read his response, I replied to him with why I'd asked for it. I said I knew that since I was the creator of this content it could be that it looked better to me than it was in reality. (see the Endowment Effect). That I needed an outside view about if the stuff on there was of any value.

But that was, even if it had some truth to it, mostly a symptom of a deeper problem. A problem so big I thought if I hadn't taken preventative measures against then that it hadn't happened yet, it was going to possibly, so possibly, be the end of me continuing to do this as freely as I do, or to me forgetting why I started writing.

While I was right about the endowment effect; how much value we as humans can irrationally give to our possessions, or worse, our own 'creations' (physical or not), I wasn't right about wanting to impress the reader. And even if it might be hard to admit, yes, that was the chief reason as to why I asked . It was really subtle, the want to do that. Masked under the innocent urge to get an 'outside view'.

I was fishing for likes. Now what if all those from whom I asked for 'feedback' , for their subjective lack of interest in my area of writing, told me I was posting fluff. Would I quit? So what if others found it important (to them, even if they didn't add that personal detail) and said to me it was?

Its actually funny what got me to my keyboard typing this. I was thinking about what to write next when I caught myself trying to constrain my sources to what was liked. This wasn't a strange thing at all. It's happened to me before, I have read about it from one of Ryan Holiday's books Ego is The Enemy (good book on ego). Yes, It's ego. It can slowly and silently replace the grounded noble reasons as to why we start out doing what we do and before we notice we are long off course, we've don't recall why we started.

Remedy was simple. I had to put in writing the reasons as to why I started. To grow a habit of reading as a pre writing ritual every time I get down to write. I mean, that's a very good way to maximize chances I'll stick to them. The alternative, trusting my brain will always know to reference them from memory without me consciously reminding myself wasn't working. It rarely does anyway.

My why or why's from most important to least ;

  1. I am writing because it encourages me to read more, which fuels the writing. So I need to read, therefore I write.
  2. I writing first to improve my writing
  3. I am writing to become more articulate in speech
  4. I am writing to have the effect I would from external advice, by reading to myself my advice like it was from someone else. (read here to see what I mean)
  5. I am writing because, someone out there might find it useful, and that's a good thing. I have learnt a lot , really, from reading others' work. I'd be glad if anyone, even one person, gained one thing from this.
  6. * (Ill keep this card from you for now)

I found it really interesting how most times, while starting out doing whatever I set out to, impressing anyone is last if at all one of the reasons why I start. I started taking photos for fun and as a meditative practice, then the likes got me chasing nice looking photos for more likes. There's a lot of friends I joined initially as a Bible thumping Christian, then eventually started consciously hiding my Bible and doing what they liked.

And that ain't impression by the way. There is no impression on the list. I think I am likening helping others (which is about them benefitting and not me) to impressing others (which would feed my ego and make me feel so good about myself.)

By the way, that contrast in the last two lines, came to mind only while I was typing the lines that came before them. I was like, wait, really, do I think by impressing others I am helping them or is that what I want it to look like? While on the inside things are totally different. Subtle , subtle.. ego so subtle..

Anyway, I found it interesting how much a little self awareness can do towards the ends of keeping us in line, if we are honest with ourselves. How I could lie to myself I was trying to help while in actuality trying to feed my pride. (there's a number 6, but its still too subtle for my self awareness)

This thinking made me more grounded in my response to compliments, or one I am still trying to have as the default.
