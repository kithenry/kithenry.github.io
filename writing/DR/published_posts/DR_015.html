Outsource your manager role, be only an employee

E015 | 25.04.25

I have always wondered why I found it way easier following my own advice when I read it from some board. That is regardless of whether it was me that put it up that board. Or say, got it from any external source. Or let's just say, had the feeling it wasn't from me.

That it was more instructive, there was less friction taking action, when I had it said to me what I had to do than if I said it to myself.. Say through some recording or reading my own old work. And it was interesting learning it wasn't only me. That there was some interesting psychological explanation for it. KInda like the principal-agent stuff from business but a bit different.

We have a lot of people today, and I think we have always had, that give really good advice. They know exactly what has to be done. I mean, really. They can draw out for you the path to your goal including the possible hiccups that might try to block you from it. They'll give you advice on how to swerve these also.

But... a good number of the same lot if not a majority can't live up to the standards required to hit these goals, if they had them in their own lives. That the numbers would increase, of those that hit their goals, among this lot, if they got mentors (of whichever form). Even if the mentors regurgitated to them exactly the same stuff they used to tell others.

I think that's why the mentor, coach business thrives so well. It ain't easy to be your own manager. To be a manager and an employee at the same time I mean. It's really easy to give direction if you ain't the one to follow it. It's I could say, easier to follow instructions, if they are from some perceived authority. And it's easier to be concerned with only either one of doing or managing action.

As the manager, you are responsible for hitting your personal goals. You know what has to be done, and why it has to be done. You know there's temptations that'd stop you from getting things done, know that would derail you from the goal. So you set strategies to make sure you don't fall prey to these. You give all the rules that'd be needed so the ultimate goal gets hit.

You also have to worry if the goals will get hit. If the employees will comply. If you are doing things the right way.

As an employee, you have your own struggles. Social media, watching movies, outright laziness, insecurities, a need to be autonomous. You just have all this personal stuff you got to fight so you can do your thing, follow rules set forward by the manager. Make sure you get done what tasks the manager assigns to you.

Now if you are both, your goals (the manager's and the employee's) are on a collision course. You don't think you can trust the manager. Because you know it's the incapable you that's running things, so you often impeach him and run things. You don't have any problems breaking the rules, because it's your rules so you can break them whenever. There's that feeling that even when there's a manager, there's not one really, it's just you. So you can make your own rules. That is, you can be without any.

The Manager is upset you never get things done. You always choose the easy path even when you know what path you had to take. You are lazy. You need to be fired.

So you are as a whole internally set against yourself in some sense. The result? Collision, and then? and then an explosion. It's that feeling of Internal conflict that eventually results in a collapse of your systems.

Solution time. I like how we can stand outside ourselves as humans and look at ourselves take on these multiple sub personalities, while we make the judgements. The stuff of introspection. Anyway. So the chief problem here, I think, is the difficulty to be accountable to our ownselves.

Here's how to do that. NOPE, here HOW I DO THAT;

Journaling. When reading your own journal it should have you feeling like some sage is passing down gems of wisdom to YOU. That feeling someone's watching over you. I think that was the whole point of Marcus Aurelius' letters. They read like they were to someone other than himself. Like they were to the reader. But that was the exact point. He was the reader. There's articles out there on how to start journaling.

Get a mentor. SOmeone that's going to reduce your worries about what might go wrong because they have seen most of what can. Tell you what has to be done, for they have been down the same path as you are and are on the other side. Or they are further down the path you are trekking. It feels good having someone who whenever you feel you won't be able to, gives you the reason why you can.

If you can't find a physical mentor for some of your life domains, like me, read books. Maybe stick to this one author and read their books so often you start to think they are right their holding your hand as you progress towards whichever goal.
