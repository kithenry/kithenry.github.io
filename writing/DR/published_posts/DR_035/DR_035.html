On a grounded definition for `enough`

E035|15.05.25

I have enough of item X. My neighbour bought a new item X, it is more expensive than mine. But what does that mean about my satisfaction with my item X? Nothing. It's not defined by my neighbour's posessions. I have enough of item X.

Or,

I am at level X, I am grateful for where I am. But I know I can do better. I am progressing at rate Z to the next level. I notice my neighbour is at level Y. Level Y is further ahead than level X. But what does that mean about what I should do regards my satisfaction with my position? Nothing. It ain't relative to Y's position. I am still progressing at rate Z to the next level. My neighbour could probably help me if I'm facing some issues with my progress. But I am not. I am progressing at rate Z.

That's a line of thinking I am trying to adopt to keep me grounded. I think without this my state of stability is bound to be in a fluctuating state depending on who has more or less of something than I do. There's a kind of peace from having a grounded intrinsic definition of enough.
