On how much power we there is in choosing what we focus on

E028|08.05.25

Yesterday's post on returning often to one's guiding principle might be interestingly similar to today's.

I was ,like yesterday, thinking today about the effect what I focused on had on my perception of the world around me. How I had it wrong somehow in my mind that where my eyes were focused determined the serious business that all other eyes had to be looking at.

And that's how I started proving myself wrong. I learnt that my eyes' major focus was a reflection mostly of me and not of something external if anything at all. That if I always had in my X daily feed a curation listing all murderings from all over the planet, that I thought murders were happening too often 'of recent' and I needed to start taking security measures was not relevant to deciding if there had been any changes in murder stats.

Or that if in my self irresponsible circles everyone was pouring out reasons why racism was to blame for X, Y and Z, I wasn't the most rational to as a result start seeing it everywhere. My eye's focus, my thoughts led me to the conclusion, had a huge influence on what I tricked myself both into believing was major and into seeing that major thing more common. And that was regardless of whether what I thought was major was or if what I saw there was actually there.

I connected that to the next thought. This was from a memory node the previous thought had made glow. I remembered how in one Mr. Money Mustache entry, Mr. Money talked about how so much of the reality we lived in was a creation of our own minds. The employee that saw all reasons as to why they were going to be fired. The student that was convinced the school was against his kind and was all out its business of failing him or some variation of that. Must have been the optimisim gun.

Interesting how it led me to an optimistic thought. That if I could most times, subconciously be gotten to look at things as important that weren't in actuality by adjustment in how frequenctly they crossed my eyes (social media, social circles), how could I take that power and use it to my benefit.

I thougt about how I could create a reality that made it easier for me to reach my goals. How I could be more rational in a world thriving on fear and anxiety, by taking control of how often I looked at the news (that's something I have been doing already for a while now), how I could be more optimistic by focusing on what was within my control and therefore get max ROI on my focus.

That was a good thought break I think.
