On the value of self assessment

E030|10.05.25 
### On contemplating our true capabilities

Inspiration: March 2nd Entry | The Daily Stoic by Ryan Holiday

#### Intro
Reading an entry from the daily stoic on self assessment got me thinking about what I was truly capable of. What I knew I was capable of. If that was all I was able to do. And for what I thought I couldn't, if I really couldn't. Here's some of the points from the reflection. 

#### On how adaptable we are
I was first reflecting on various events that have happened before, which, before they happened, I thought they were going to be the end of me. How even with those thoughts towards quite a number of situations I have been through, here I am. There was a lot of examples my mind ran through. 

I remember the times when I had to change school. My mind went something like: "Will I be able to cope when I change school, my life will for ever change when I join a new school for sure!" And then when I did, I fit in so well I started having the same thoughts about what would happen if I had to joing another one. 

Or on losing close contact with friends due to change in location: "Oh, I am losing my friends! will i be able to make new ones? Life is going to be a huge mess right after I part from them. How will I cope?" And shortly after the split I was so lost in my daily routines there was not as much thought about the friends I lost as I thought there would be. 

From this I learnt how much I'd under estimated my own capability to cope with life. How capable we are as humans of coping. I think someone that's lost a close relative would find this sentiment most relatable.

#### On the limitless potential to improve
It wasn't only about the ability to cope that I thought. I also pondered parts of my life that I have improved through conscious effort to do so over the years. How much I have gained from admitting my own insufficiencies. That was a long list. This led to the next thought;

What then was stopping me facing my flaws in areas of my life where I was? What is it that was so special about these flaws that they couldn't, like all the rest, be cleared if not brought down to their very minimum, like I'd managed to do with the others? This thought was optimistic. I mean , how cool is that! That for every flaw I have, its a possible self improvement project. If I want to be better where I think I am insufficient, why then should should I keep denying my flaws, the ones I deny, and in so doing, sell my self short on self improvment?

#### Close 
I learnt how much we miss for a lack of self assessment. This is all I thought about for the time I gave to the exercise but I am sure there is more. I only listed capabilities here but I think there's also limits to what we can do. Limits that we fail to admit to our own demise. Food for thought.
