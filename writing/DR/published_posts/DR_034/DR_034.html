On having a personal code

E034|15.05.25

It was thinking about how I would stay grounded in what I've read long after I've read it. How to increase the chances each principle I picked from every book of me became one of the things that defined my personality. And, I think I finally have an answer. I knew about having a personal code of conduct before, but had never known why I'd ever need one.

Till I felt I needed to cut down on my reading to manage some responsibilities. But how was I going to stay grounded without daily reading I thought to myself. I know how I always drift a few days after I quit daily self help reading. I need something I can read daily that is a composite of simple rules that can without me revisiting all the books I have read so far, guide me like it was all those books guiding me.

And I think a personal code does exactly that. I think it will do that. It is a work in progress but good enough to be functional for now. Tried looking online for templates on what to include but mostly found business related info. Code of ethics and the sort. Anyhow. Just writing a few rules and I was like, yes, this is what I have been looking for. Why didn't I think of this earlier.

For I anticipate times when reading a book will be a luxury I can't afford but I don't think I'll ever be so packed I can't carry a sheet of paper or a page or two long pdf with my code. (length varies as your code grows). Another thing I thought about is , will the code be an image of me. I was like, nope. It will however be an image of the ideal me. The perfect me. But I am human and i can't be perfect. Which meant, my success daily would be to be as close to my code as I can be.

I had to tell myself I was going to have to read it at least every morning. At best, have it always with me so I can add some rules as I grow. For it's an effective way, like I have written in an earlier article, that I have learnt to make something influece my actions.
