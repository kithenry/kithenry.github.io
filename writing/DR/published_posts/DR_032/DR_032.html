On eating your cake and having it.

E032|12.05.25

Inspiration : March 9th Entry | The Daily Stoic , Ryan Holiday

I think I've read about this even from the Bible before. That the cost to do right will at times be losing relations you have known to be your only for the most part of your life. Sometimes your friends, or worse, your own parents. Interesting to find the same sentiment echoed by Epictetus in Discourses 4.2.1. He says to the reader that he must choose whether to be loved by his friends and remain the same person or become better at the cost of losing them. But there's a third option, and it never works. It's having both the friends that are the reason for the person you are trying to not be and being a new person. At such cross roads, I am pushed to consider which one I prefer over the other. Being liked or growing up. Bad company corrupts good morals Paul says in the NT.

With whom do you consort? Have you any concern what influence they have on you? Do you stand for anything? Questions to ponder.
