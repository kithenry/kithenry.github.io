On Moral Standards and Boundaries

E022 | 02.05.25

I was thinking about people I met with whom we had different definitions for what was acceptable and what wasn't. It wasn't about those who believed differently than I did as it relates to religion, but you could say that is a subset of this thought I was having.

I was wondering why I got frustrated with a friend who used four-letter words or similar as casually as mentioning a neutral word like pen or pencil and could not get themselves to stop when talking to people who didn't. When talking to me. Writing the two last lines alone told me part of why I was wrong.

It turned on some voice in my head that was screaming, "Who do you think you are, referring to yourself in the third person and expecting people to adjust to you just by you being around them?"

Anyway, I always get frustrated when I have to get someone to, if I can, not ask certain questions or expect me to be interested in certain topics when I am talking to them. Because it's disgusting, and I wouldn't want to get accustomed to the same (a high possibility if I am in such circles for long enough).

But I was like, I think they do it because first:

That's normal to them

They do have things they consider unacceptable, but that's not one of those things. I think that's how they have decided to live their lives, and I am in no position to expect them to act differently when talking to me without me requesting them to.

Now I personally always feel convicted about things I do that I know are wrong but have grown accustomed to and therefore find normal (probably the same way we all get accustomed to doing things we know are wrong) when around people whose character is against such things. This thought gave me the second point.

It could be my character says I am tolerant of such topics

It could be the case that it's due to what conversations I have with my friends that they subconsciously read from me a character that has no issue talking about certain topics or using certain language. Or three:

I just have never ever bothered to tell them

What is punished is not repeated. What is not punished, even when it is not rewarded, will most often be repeated. If I tell someone that I don't talk about topic X because I am a Christian, the first time they bring it up, chances are high they won't ever bring such topics to me and will spread the word. The problem will be solved. No silent resentment. But if I always supplement what they are saying with more interesting such stories as they present to me, even when I know they aren't right, why should I expect them to think I am not interested? I am, by so doing, drawing for them my character, who I am (which is my actions).

I think the central definition for my character is my religion. I mean, it should be that, ideally. But... Is it?

Being Christian is not going to get anyone to be convicted when I am with them. Acting Christian is the only thing that will. So I was like, well, I think this is all because I am not acting Christian enough, if at all. Because I am being the light that is hidden under the bed. The light that is as bright as the darkness it was sent to drive out.

I started off with, this isn't about religion, but surprise, surprise, it ended with Christianity.

I am not sure what religion you belong to but I think the concept goes even beyond religion. Oh, by the way, disclaimer. I believe Chrisitanity is the only true religion. Just in case.
