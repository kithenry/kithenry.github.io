You did good. Yes you did. Now get back to work.

E014 | 24.04.25

I don't know about you but when something I did brings me success, in whichever form, I tend to get stuck in some form of loop. Constantly getting back to my trapped in 'a glass _trophy_', as if to bask in its glory.

Could be good marks, could be a write up on some platform that I think turned out well, mention it. But that's not my point. And this is the problem.

While this is happening it feels like I am a prisoner in my own body. Like, within me is some other ego-hungry sub-persona that's overridden my system, keeps me in the unproductive loop, eyes glued to the small win, and won't let me move on to the next task because...

Because I can keep getting the good feeling I seek from task completion without putting in any extra effort. All I have to do is return and return and return.

And that's something I think we've probably all faced before. Forgetting it's only a milestone when we hit one. Spending more time than we ought to acknowledging small wins. And now we are afraid of taking the next step for the fear we won't perform as 'well'.

Or we have lost momentum since we've now associated with 'doing nothing' (repeated peeks at trophy -> dopamine reward) with a reward we only had to get after 'doing something'.

How to get around this? Well, how about, how I get around this. Mine is simple. Limit how much time you spend celebrating milestones. I don't know what small wins you can't help coming to. What domain they are from.

But mine's something like. Post something, looks good. Cool. Done. Forgotten. Next. Paper done, what was good, what was bad, cool , forgotten, next (this example aligns more with rumination than it does with the central theme of this post but ..).

It can be real hard to not take an extra peek at mini trophy. It takes discipline obviously. But considering what's at stake for you, it's worth the effort. It is for me.
