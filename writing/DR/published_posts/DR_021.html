Your Word's Worth

E012 | 01.05.2025

Quote: A man's word is his bond.

I was reflecting on the meaning of this and what strength my word had if any. I was thinking about what implications having a weak word has had on me, the implications of not being a man of my word. I think I have always felt honorable for the promises I made to myself, when I kept them. That I felt I could do even more, that I was in some sense competent, because , I did as I had told myself I would.

On the other hand, for phases in my life when my word was fluctuating, which is unfortunately most of my life, there was a feeling of worthlessnes. There still is when I do so. A broken word meant more broken words. Still dones. It made me feel less and less capable I would ever get anything done. Still results in the same. Felt like a big shame. Still embarassing. That's an ugly feeling.

I say I'll post a blog update every day, then I quit, I'll use my phone for only 2 hours a day, then I quit, I'll send a friend a text every morning, then I quit, I'll only talk to you during this time, then I am available all day every day, I'll grab something for you when I come back from home, then for some worthless reason I change my mind. There's so many I don't think I ever keep track. And that's I think a probable

I feel like its been small small broken promises like these, not may big ones, that when they built up created a strong feeling of insufficiency. Of weakness. And I think its not only me that looks at myself like this when I blunder in this manner.

I am sure we are all constantly studying each other as humans, almost subconsicously. So I think all that I think of myself, those that are in my circle think the same of me. Or act in ways that reflect I have not been a man of my word, if that's who I have been to them.

Now how does a man strengthen his word. Haven't looked at any external sources yet. Only inferred from what I already have.

He who is faithful in the little will be trusted with the much. That's a verse from the Bible.

I was like, maybe I can start by following a few simple rules. Its not desirable to be stable in one life domain and unstable in all the rest. I think I would like to have stability across. Some code which reigned over all my life domains. I'd trust someone that even when they never promised much, whenever they did, it was 100% guarantee only death would stop them.

I'll start with things I have heard over and over before but haven't thought much about applying;

  • Never make any promise you won't keep. (And that's both for the promises to yourself and those to others)
    • Make the simplest promises you are very sure you will for your ability to and be good with that.
    • I think word strength is a muscle, kind of like a virtue that can be grown gradually with simple rules like these. No promises, small promises, a growing definition of small.
  • Do what you set out to do no matter what the cost is
    • Thinking about this reminded me of a story I recently read from the Old Testament.
    • Jephthah promised God he would offer up to Him as a burnt offering the first person that would welcome him when he came back from war if God granted him victory. The first person to come out to celebrate Jephthah's victory was the daughter and he did as he had said. That's crazy. That's honorable.

I thought it was interesting how breaking or living by only these two rules can be the reason why a man is seen as worthless or another as honourable.
