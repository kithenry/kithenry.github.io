Aiming at a title for those that do good work Vs doing good work

E019 | 29.04.25

The two can seem deceptively similar but they can be very different. A very good number, you could say most but I can't access the stats, have their guns pointed at the titles for producers of good work. The rest, a relatively smaller number, want to do good work. How are the two different...

Let's look at just that. First, at what results from the one path, doing good work, then at wanting to be identified as someone that's good at doing good work.

You are aiming at becoming the kind who produces quality work, good work.

You are likely going to get the title for it

I mean, you are producing actually good work, and you have actual confidence from the experience you have garnered doing actual training. It ain't hard to notice that kind of people. You'll be noticed. But will you get the title? Maybe.... maybe not. The world ain't your father's home. Humans ain't angels. So, who knows, maybe you are in a fair environment and good work gets rewarded. Who knows, a title is one of the rewards you are given, if any.

But on the good side;

You are going to be an actual expert

You did put in the work to be able to produce such good results, and you know it. You did not skip any steps since it was about knowing the field inside out that you were concerned. You respected the discipline you undertook, and you maybe did it because you liked it. After years of that kind of mindset you can be sure of proficiency. Some master in your field, 1% of the 1% you will be making new rules for how things must be done in it.

But what's more interesting is;

A title won't phase you

Since your satisfaction was from producing good work, the titles won't be anything to you. Only extra. And that's something to be proud of. How hard it is to not want to be. To not be egotistical. How honoring it is to see not much value in what you deserve, what those that don't deserve would kill for. Imagine for yourself.

 

On the other hand, if you just want to become someone. To get the respect without putting in the work it takes to get to the respected title;

You'll burn yourself out trying and probably won't get there,

You'll lose your values if you have any

Its often ego driven people, terribly ambitious about becoming someone, and not concerned at all about the work that would kill for a title. The actual work needs effort, the work needs patience, the work needs resilience in the face of failure, the work takes time. But the dire need for a title has never been with a person that's willing to do all that, a patient person. So, what would stop them from taking the pragmatic route. Using the paths that work? I mean the paths do work don't they. If you opt for the ambitious path, you surely will, gradually, start to widen your range of what's acceptable, to include what leads fastest to the title you so very much desire. What that will be, I don't know.

You'll be a charlatan

Since it's hard that you'll ever be ready to rack up the experience that you'd need to be an actual {fill in the gap}, you'll fake it. But, like Simon the Sorcerer from the Bible, you'll never make it. We already have such figures as these in different fields I am sure you know. That's a good number of the experts tv watchers are over and over told to trust. Folk that only know how to tie a tie, put on good suits and pose for the camera. Sorry for the long tangent. But the list is still going...

You'll never be secure

It can be easy to pose when in a room of people you know don't know any better. People that for some reason know less than you do or think that that is the case, and you know it. But that changes for the worse when you are surrounded by actual experts, or, should I say, folk you think to be actual experts (There might be charlatans that have you as their safe space, folk that think of them as experts). But anyway, you won't help fearing you might be spotted because deep down you know you don't belong. Your papers are ... wait you don't have any papers. And if it's worth mentioning anyway

You'll never be an actual expert

Because allow me to go in circles here please... because you are not... full stop.

Now it'll help you to think about what you are doing and why you are doing it. Maybe its the title that you want, maybe you want to be an actual expert, so you take the path that leads to that. Whichever case, it helps not to confuse the paths for the two destinations to be one in the same.
