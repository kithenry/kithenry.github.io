The systems are running, be a good boy and chill ..

E011 | 21.04.25

I like to think of the life governing systems we build for ourselves ( for those that do ) as imaginary managers. And, a combo of all these under one system as the general manager. Each of which could be a set of rules to ensure consistent standards and, make it so that desired results always come out on the other end.

So it's like when we plan and make systems we assign the work of worrying and deciding in real time what step we should take next to this general manager, which we do from learning how frustrating it can be having to always be making decisions and worrying what will work.

But like the micromanager who, after assigning sub ordinates tasks wants to be over their heads 'overriding the system' and taking things in his own hands, we often fall into the same trap when we override our own systems and take back the burden of constant worry and real time decision making.

It's decided you have to do X like so but you want to think again if you should do it. It's drawn out the rules will lead to the goal but you burden yourself there after with thoughts about if things will work.. It's interesting how hard it can be to let go and follow even the rules we make for ourselves.
