What is appreciated, it appreciates. And the inverse .... you know how it goes

E013 | 23.04.25

I remember the first self help master I had. Jim Rhon. Great guy in his own respect. He had a lot of great sayings. This' one of those that stuck with me to date. For how relatable it was, still is.

Interesting how someone, after they've been doing something trivially, they start to put in effort towards doing it even more and doing it even better , now that they get this appreciation for it.

That certain actions they do, if they are appreciated, appreciate. As in, they happen more often.

But what's even more interesting I think is how equally debilitating a lack of appreciation can be. I know views about this might be a bit different from a Stoic perspective but, assuming all other factors kept constant. We can really be discouraged or quit carrying on with putting in the proverbial reps for thoughts what we are doing is going unnoticed and no one would ever notice even if we stopped.

We all have those figures in life. I do. Figures that do a lot for us but whose many good actions, for familiarity, we see right through like they are not there. Maybe we do notice but don't think its worth our time to vocalise that to them. Mayve something else.

But, what if it's that appreciation that's going to be the reason they keep going ?The reason they quit plans to quit. Maybe it won't feel so good if they got that praise after they quit. 'Where were you all this time? Why didn't you tell me while I was still active?' is what reaction I think they might have for post quits appreciation.

It's like you are preparing to water a withered plant. Because while it's still up and strong and providing you with fruit. All you care for it satisfying yourself with its fruit while it ever more quietly dies from the inside. Eventually its dead for a lack of care, and, here you are with your watering can. "Oh, plant, here's some water for you to drink, I care for you you know. I always have."

Same will be with the person from whom you are holding any of that appreciation. I think we know what is wise to do, what we would want others to do for us.
