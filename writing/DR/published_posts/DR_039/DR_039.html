On the power to attach polarity

E039|19.05.25

The stone I trip on and fall. The bird in flight that excretes while it passes right over my head. The bomb that is sent by whomever to where I live. All these things and events are equally indifferent to me. And I have to treat them as equally so. At least in principle. Just like I would, as a sane being, not fight the rain for falling while my clothes are out but take them in and get on with my business, I should when I fall, refrain from cursing whoever planted a stumbling block in my way, dust my self and walk. When someone throws insults directed at me, I should give their words the same attention I give to car traffic sounds from the street. When something happens, I should take sometime to think before rushing to attach polarity (good or bad). I should remind myself of the third polarity. The Neutral. For neutralising. I should remind myself that I have the power to attach polarity. I should learn to wield it.
