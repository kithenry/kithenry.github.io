On how often to return to guiding principles.

E027|08.05.25

I was thinking about how much influence what we focus on has on our perception of the world around us. How we can by misplacing our focus, and focusing on what's not within our control, it can loom so large within our minds and get us forgetting about what is.. Which should objectively be given more weight.

Thinking about this led me to pondering on how books are able to change our perception about certain aspects of life. How a book on courage is able to get someone to do courageous deeds. I remember that personally duing the time when I was reading Ryan Holiday's Courage is Calling. That by visiting this book as often as I did, I started to give less weight to the strength of what was stopping me from doing what I was afraid of if it was strong at all, and more to the ability I had to face my fears.

I noticed one thing. That how often I returned to these principles so much influenced what effect they had on me. Principles on courage, principles on good financial habits, principles on a good Spiritual life... principles on anything good.

I noticed that the times when I backslid in any of the domains of my life I was seeking to be stronger in, there was a correlating backslide in how often I was reading material that had potential to keep me grounded.

That to keep growing, to keep the good benefits that there are if any from my guiding principle, I ought to return to it as often as is possible.
