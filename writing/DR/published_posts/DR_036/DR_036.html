On precision with language.

E036|16.05.25

I had to do it. Nope, I chose to do it.

I can't do that. Nope, I can't get myself to do that. Nope, I choose not to put in the effort needed to do that.

That is scary. Nope, I am scared of that.

I failed that. Nope, that attempt was not successful. It was a failed attempt.

I failed that. Your phone is good. Nope, I like your phone.

I have a good phone. Nope, I have a phone that is good for me. Nope, I have a phone that is good enough for me. Nope, I have a phone that is good enough for my current use case.

I like my phone because it is the best. Nope, I like my phone is better than most of others' phones. Nope, I like my phone because I think it is better than most of others' phones. Nope, I like my phone because it makes me feel better than most.

It is a hard subject. Nope, it is a hard subject for me. Nope, I can't get myself to put in the work that subject needs for me to learn it. Nope, I choose not to put in the effort that subject needs for me to learn it.

That is frustrating. Nope, I am frustrated by that. Nope, I choose to be frustrated by that.

I don't know if it's only I that sees the shift in meaning with increased precision. I attempted to level that up with every level of break down and hope I did exactly that. It's interesting how much bondage there is in something as simple as the words and phraseology we without knowledge of their effect use in our day to day speech.
