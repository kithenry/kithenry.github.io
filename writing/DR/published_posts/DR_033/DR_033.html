On mastering the mind.

E033|14.05.25

So I was going through my old journal entries today and found something that, to me, was motivational. I'll share it here so I can refine it more and build discussions around it.

Diary entry from the 27th of October last year 2024. I remember around that time I was reading David Goggins' 'Can't Hurt Me'

Endurance, staying in the game for as long as you actually can, which is way longer than your mind convinces you. And that's most true amidst extreme discomfort and when in face with odds so bleak you wonder how you'll be able to keep on moving. DG's given me something I think if I meditated on for long enough, if I practiced for long enough, I'd be headed for great things. He says there's always that moment while doing a strenous task that you feel so exhausted you start to contemplate quitting (which is failing). DG says it is exactly then that you have the best chance to break barriers and find out the actual limits to what you can do. To unlock the next level. Which almost always is the case, that we can do more.

He says within is a governor of sorts (like a speed governer in a car) which if we don't consciously control it, can limit our potential to as low as 30% of our actual capacity. And I've tested this in reality during my morning runs (inspired by DG). I have learnt how optimistic it is that we can smile while we are at our lowest and feel like quitting.

That because it is then that we break levels and advance. That it is then that we most need to stay in the game, for most of times we find out it was all in our heads and that in actuality, we could do better. We ,after successfully swatting such thoughts as these when they occur, can like David Goggins, start wondering what Challenge there is if it exists that we can't solve.

I wrote this whole article while I struggled to keep my eyes open. It helped me re discover a lot of my tiredness was a mind thing.
