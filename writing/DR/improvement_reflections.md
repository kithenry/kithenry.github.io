### 🎯 Points of Improvement

#### 1. Tone
- Some posts (e.g., DR_019) sound **preachy**—almost entirely based on reading, not experience.
- I should:
  - [ ] Use more curious language (e.g., *"From what I’ve read…"*, *"Experts suggest…"*)
  - [ ] Add citations where appropriate
  - [ ] Invite input from expert readers
  - [ ] Avoid presenting ideas as if I’m an authority

#### 2. Point of View
- Posts DR_011 to DR_019 shift between “you” and “we” inconsistently.
- Since this is a **personal reflections blog**, I’ll stick to:
  - **“I”** point of view as default
  - Journal-style tone
  - Reader engagement at the end via soft prompts:
    - *“What’s your take?”*, *“Have you ever felt this too?”*, etc.

#### 3. Structure
- Too early to define a fixed structure.
- For now, structure = paragraphing.
- Aim for consistency via:
  - [ ] Opening with a thought/trigger
  - [ ] Ending with an invitation to reflect

#### 4. Content / Niche
- I reflect on different things: productivity, philosophy, religion, etc.
- No fixed niche for now. The niche is:
  > **My perspective on whatever I reflect on deeply.**
- Action:
  - [ ] Use tags to track recurring themes and patterns

#### 5. Frequency
- Writing daily. It has worked before, but also led to burnout (Substack era).
- Current environment feels healthier:
  - No pressure to optimize homepage
  - No chasing likes or metrics
  - No forced formatting
- Concerns:
  - [ ] Fear of writer’s block, late posts, or future burnout
  - [ ] Need to make the writing system efficient enough to support daily posting
