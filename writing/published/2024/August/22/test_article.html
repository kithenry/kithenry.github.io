Speech kills action.

12-11-2024

Speaking and Action vi for the same resources -- Ryan Holiday

Could be about what you are planning to do, about how you will do it, or about how much progress you've made. Maybe you are compelled to do it for how impressive you think you'll look to others for having big plans. Yes there's always an urge ever so strong to yap but just stop doing it! Stop boasting!!

Like it is with any vice, anyone can do it. Anyone can give in to the urge to talk and talk and trust me they will never lack an audience, howbeit an undescerning one. There's always something about a vice that keeps people coming back to it. For boasting, when we talk about something we haven't done yet, It as if seems to us it's now done. I mean, we've already gotten the praise we could have from having released finished work. What then is the point in getting down to put those words into action?

There's a lot of interesting scientific material written on the matter, books written on this, on how debilitating talk can be, on the fact they, that is both talking and action, compete for the same resources. But what's more honorable you think? Yep, it's the hard thing. To focus solely on action.

It should not surprise you then that the biggest talkers almost always amount to just that, big talkers.

Isn't it though more honourable to have the self control to keep quiet. For how rare it is...
